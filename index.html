The acceleration of digital engagement in the post-pandemic world shows no sign of slowing down. Twilio, the leading cloud communications and customer engagement platform, spoke with over 3,000 leading decision makers and 4,500 consumers to understand how the digital landscape has changed over the past year and reveal the challenges organizations are set to face.

The State of Customer Engagement Report 2022 reveals that digital transformation has made a significant impact — increasing revenue by 70% on average for companies that made the investment in customer engagement. Unsurprisingly, these same companies expect their investment to almost double by 2025.

However, the fast pace of change also means that businesses will need to quickly adapt. Personalization is now essential for customer retention and data protection has taken on greater importance amongst consumers. And consumers’ views on these topics diverge sharply from what companies believe.

Another warning sign: digital fatigue is starting to affect engagement, especially among younger demographics. Companies that act now to create more meaningful digital engagement will offset this trend and will see benefits, in terms of both revenue and customer engagement, in the long term.

Executive Summary

Our research revealed that organizations that put digital customer engagement at the forefront of their business have reaped outsize rewards. This growth has its challenges, because consumers don’t always see things the same way as the brands marketing to them. Companies that can close that gap now have the chance to become leaders in digital transformation, creating market-leading data practices that entice consumers and keep them loyal.

These are the five commandments of customer engagement which emerged from our deep investigation of both consumer and B2C companies’ attitudes.

Embrace digital engagement and massive ROI will follow.70% — that’s the average revenue increase among companies that invested in digital customer engagement.

Better personalization is essential — and there’s room for improvement.88% of businesses say personalization is extremely or very important to their overall strategy. Consumers, though, still want companies to improve in this area.

A cookieless world is here — the time to get ready is now.81% of companies are completely or substantially reliant on third-party cookies — even though this critical data source will not be available after 2022. A new approach is needed, one that relies on data companies own themselves.

Consumers don’t trust companies with their data, and they demand better.95% of B2C companies believe consumers trust their ability to protect data, yet only 65% of consumers actually do trust these companies.

Digital fatigue is real. Focus on smarter engagement, not more interactions.Younger millennial and Gen Z consumers are increasingly fatigued by too much screen time. Creating more meaningful interactions can ensure business doesn’t suffer as a result.

Methodology

Twilio engaged Lawless Research to conduct a blind survey between December 28 2021 and January 28 2022. There were a total of 3,450 respondents from 12 countries.

Respondents were full time employees at companies with 500 or more employees and at director level or above in a variety of marketing, customer support, data analytics, product, sales, and business development roles.

Each respondent was familiar with their organization’s customer experience strategy, digital customer engagement strategy, marketing strategy and marketing technology investment strategy.
